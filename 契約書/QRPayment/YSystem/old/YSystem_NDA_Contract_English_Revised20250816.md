## IV. Confidential Information

For the purposes of this Agreement, "Confidential Information" means only such information that meets the following requirements:

**(a) For written or other tangible materials**
Only information that is clearly marked "CONFIDENTIAL" (or equivalent) at the time of disclosure shall be considered confidential information.

**(b) For oral or visual disclosure**
Information disclosed orally or visually must be identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and confirmed in a written notice clearly marked "CONFIDENTIAL" within ten (10) business days after the initial disclosure, or it shall not be treated as confidential information.

Confidential Information may include, by way of example and without limitation, the following, provided they meet the above requirements (a) or (b):
- Documents, records, information and data (whether oral, electronic, or written) specifically related to the proprietary business operations of a Party or this specific project
- Drawings, models, devices, sketches, designs, schedules, product plans, marketing plans
- Analyses, compilations, research, software, prototypes, samples, patent applications, experimental results, specifications
- Other business information provided to the other Party and/or the other Party's affiliates, employees, officers, owners, agents, consultants, or representatives in the course of the work contemplated by this Agreement

However, Confidential Information does not include:

(a) Information that is generally available to the public  
(b) Widely used programming practices, algorithms, or general methodologies  
(c) Information rightfully possessed by a Party prior to entering into this Agreement  
(d) Information independently developed without using the provided confidential information  
(e) General know-how, formulations, or technical procedures commonly used in the software development industry  
(f) Information that becomes available to the public without breach of this Agreement

## V. Obligations

The obligations of both Parties shall be to hold and maintain Confidential Information in the strictest confidence at all times and to disclose it only to their agents, employees, representatives, affiliates, and other individuals or entities on a "need to know" basis.

Each Party shall be liable only for breaches caused by its own employees, agents, or subcontractors acting under its direction, provided it exercised commercially reasonable controls.

Neither Party may disclose, reproduce, or use Confidential Information for its own benefit without the written approval of the other Party. When requested, either Party shall be obligated to return all materials to the requesting Party as soon as possible.

This provision applies equally to both Parties as this is designated as a mutual agreement in Section II.

## VI. Term

The obligation of the bound Party to hold Confidential Information in confidence shall remain in effect for **five (5) years** from the date of each disclosure, unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing. Either Party may be released from this obligation by written notice from the disclosing Party.

## VII. Integration Clause

This Agreement expresses the complete understanding of both Parties regarding the subject matter and supersedes all prior proposals, agreements, representations, and understandings. This Agreement may be modified only by writing approved by both Parties.

## VIII. Severability

If a court determines that any provision of this Agreement is invalid or unenforceable, the remainder of this Agreement shall be interpreted to best reflect the intentions of both Parties.

## IX. Enforcement

Both Parties acknowledge and agree that, because of the unique and sensitive nature of the Confidential Information, any breach of this Agreement by either Party would cause the non-breaching Party irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone would be insufficient. Accordingly, upon such breach, either Party shall be entitled to pursue all remedies available at law or in equity—including, without limitation, injunctive relief and compensatory damages—to protect its interests.

## X. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and the High Court of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region shall have exclusive jurisdiction over all disputes arising under this Agreement.

## Summary of Revisions

### Major Revision Items

1. **Clarification of Mutual Agreement**: Explicitly states that both parties' confidential information is protected
2. **Clear Definition of Confidential Information**: 
   - Written materials: Requires clear "CONFIDENTIAL" marking
   - Oral disclosure: Requires declaration at the time + written confirmation within 10 business days
3. **Limited Liability Scope**: Parties are liable only for breaches by those under their direction, provided commercially reasonable controls are exercised
4. **Limited Obligation Period**: 5 years from the date of each disclosure (previously effectively unlimited)
5. **Mutual Remedies**: Changed to bilateral remedy provisions
6. **Clarified Governing Law**: Hong Kong Special Administrative Region law, High Court of Hong Kong SAR exclusive jurisdiction

### Risk Mitigation Effects

- **Elimination of Unilateral Agreement Risk**: Both parties' information is equally protected
- **Clarification of Confidential Information Scope**: Reduces risk of violation due to ambiguous definitions
- **Appropriate Liability Scope**: Avoids excessive liability burden
- **Clear Time Limits**: Avoids unlimited obligation burden
- **Enhanced Legal Stability**: Reduces dispute risk through clear governing law and jurisdiction
